Two major waves transformed India's digital landscape. The Jio revolution provided ultra-cheap mobile internet, bringing a massive, new population online. Concurrently, the India Stack—a set of government-led [[concepts/digital-infrastructure|APIs for identity, payments, and security]]—digitized core citizen services like banking (UPI). This infrastructure created a fertile but complex ground for innovation, demanding products that address extreme [[concepts/market-complexity|diversity in language, culture, and purchasing power]].
