In social networks, you often must make trade-offs that degrade one user's experience to improve another's, which contradicts [[concepts/jobs-to-be-done|Jobs-to-be-Done]] thinking. For example, Facebook's "People You May Know" feature served new users needing connections, not the viewer. Effective product decisions require [[concepts/systems-thinking|systems thinking]] about all actors' incentives, not just optimizing for a single user's job.
