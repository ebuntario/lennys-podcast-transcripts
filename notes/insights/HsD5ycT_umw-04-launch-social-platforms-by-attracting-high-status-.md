Successful social platforms often bootstrap by attracting high-status users who are underserved by existing networks. These users provide initial credibility, but lasting growth comes from cultivating homegrown talent unique to the platform. This strategy creates a new "country" where newcomers can achieve status they couldn't elsewhere, fueling [[concepts/network-effects|network effects]].
