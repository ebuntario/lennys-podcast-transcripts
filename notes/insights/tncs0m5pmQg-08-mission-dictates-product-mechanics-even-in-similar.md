A company's mission fundamentally shapes its product decisions. For example, Tinder's mission ("make single life more fun") versus Hinge's ("designed to be deleted") led to different core mechanics—swiping vs. profile prompts—despite both being dating apps. This shows why some features are built and others are deliberately resisted based on the [[concepts/strategic-alignment|strategic alignment]] with the mission.
