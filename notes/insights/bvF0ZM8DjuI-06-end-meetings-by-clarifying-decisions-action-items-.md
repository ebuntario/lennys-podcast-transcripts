To ensure [[concepts/meeting-effectiveness|meeting effectiveness]], end every meeting by asking three questions: "What did we decide here?", "Who needs to do what by when?", and "Who else needs to know?". Asking "What did we decide?" often reveals differing interpretations among attendees, forcing crucial alignment. Capturing these answers creates clear [[concepts/accountability|accountability]] and prevents decisions from stalling due to poor communication.
