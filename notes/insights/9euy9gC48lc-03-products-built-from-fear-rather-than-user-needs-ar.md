When product development is motivated by competitive fear rather than a genuine desire to solve user problems, failure is almost guaranteed. Successful products like [[concepts/product-strategy|product strategy]] emerge from deep user research and a focus on creating something wonderful for people. In contrast, projects driven by fear often become privacy disasters or simply don't resonate, because they start from the wrong foundational motivation.
