Many growth and product-market fit challenges are solved not by jumping to solutions, but by [[concepts/problem-framing|asking the right question]]. Examples include "Why didn't you download the software?" or "Who considers this a must-have?" Cultivating the skill of asking obvious, context-rich questions at the right moment is a foundational element of effective [[concepts/growth-strategy|growth strategy]].
