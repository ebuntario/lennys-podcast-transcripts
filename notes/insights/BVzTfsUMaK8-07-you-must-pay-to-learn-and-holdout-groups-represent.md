Learning is not free; allocating traffic to a control group or a new variant that might fail is the cost of acquiring knowledge. The value of an [[concepts/testing|experiment]] should be judged by the information it generates for future decisions, not just the immediate impact of the winning variant. This mindset shift is necessary to justify running bold, potentially informative tests.
