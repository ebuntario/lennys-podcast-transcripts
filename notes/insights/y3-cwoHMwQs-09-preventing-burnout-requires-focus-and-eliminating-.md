Beyond offering time off and benefits, preventing burnout requires ruthless [[concepts/prioritization|prioritization]] about what to stop doing. Organizations should identify the small number of important things that must happen and eliminate unnecessary context switching. This focus, combined with flexibility around [[concepts/remote-work|remote work]] arrangements, helps maintain team health during sustained high-pressure periods.
