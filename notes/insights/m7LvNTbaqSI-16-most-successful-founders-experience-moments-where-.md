Nearly every founder reaches a point where they seriously consider shutting down their startup due to apparent failure. This near-death experience is practically universal in the [[concepts/startup-journey|startup journey]], with a significant percentage of successful companies having faced existential crises. Recognizing this commonality can help founders persist through difficult periods when hope seems lost.
