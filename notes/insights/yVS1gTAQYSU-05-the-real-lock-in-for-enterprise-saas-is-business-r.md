The enduring value of applications like Salesforce or Workday isn't their user interface or basic data model, but the years of evolved business rules and workflows configured for each specific company. Over time, the software becomes a bespoke system encoding unique company processes, creating significant lock-in. This deep configuration layer is a black box that is difficult to replicate, making it a formidable [[concepts/competitive-advantage|competitive advantage]] beyond just being a "form on a database."
