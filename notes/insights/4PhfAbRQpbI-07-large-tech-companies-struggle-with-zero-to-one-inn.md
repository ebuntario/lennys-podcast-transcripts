Large companies find it difficult to innovate from zero to one because incentives favor defending ideas with existing market signals. It's hard to get alignment on hunches or first-principles concepts that lack precedent. This creates a [[concepts/risk-aversion|risk-averse]] environment where copying existing successful products is often the only defensible path forward.
