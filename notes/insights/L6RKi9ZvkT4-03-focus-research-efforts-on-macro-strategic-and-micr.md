Research efforts should prioritize macro-level work (e.g., competitive landscape, forward-looking innovation) and micro-level work (e.g., usability testing, understanding A/B test results). The problematic "middle-range" research—like understanding how a user group feels about a feature—is often interesting but not impactful, as it yields insights that are hard to operationalize and can be dismissed as obvious. Shifting focus drives greater [[concepts/business-impact|business impact]].
