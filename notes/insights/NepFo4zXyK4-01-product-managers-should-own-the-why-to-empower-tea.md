A product manager does not need to be the sole source of ideas, but they must uniquely own the "why" behind a product decision. This clarity allows designers and engineers to make great local decisions autonomously, which is essential for scaling [[concepts/team-autonomy|team autonomy]] and product development. The "why" provides the guiding principle that aligns distributed decision-making across functions.
