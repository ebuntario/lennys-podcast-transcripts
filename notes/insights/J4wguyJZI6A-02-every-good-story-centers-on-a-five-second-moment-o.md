Every compelling story is about a singular moment of [[concepts/transformation|transformation]] or realization, where the protagonist's perspective fundamentally shifts. The purpose of the story is to bring that moment into clarity for the audience, with 98% of the narrative serving as context. This structure applies to all forms of storytelling, from personal anecdotes to major films.
