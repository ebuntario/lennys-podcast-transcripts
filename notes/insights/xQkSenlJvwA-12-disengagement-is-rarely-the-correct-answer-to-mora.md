When facing morally complex work (e.g., in defense or government), disengagement is rarely the right answer. Being in the room to improve processes—making them more accurate, targeted, and less error-prone—is often a net positive. This requires actively engaging with the [[concepts/ethical-decision-making|ethical decision-making]] and political dimensions of technology, rather than refusing to look at them.
