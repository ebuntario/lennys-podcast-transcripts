Adopt a Directly Responsible Individual (DRI) model where one person gathers written input from cross-functional partners but owns the final decision. Others must "disagree and commit," then champion the decision as if it were their own. This eliminates passive-aggressive behavior and speeds up [[concepts/decision-making|decision-making]] by making accountability clear and reducing consensus-driven delays.
