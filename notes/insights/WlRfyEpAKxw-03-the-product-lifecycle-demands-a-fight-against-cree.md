Products naturally attract users with simplicity, then add features for power users, which can alienate the core audience and push them to a newer, simpler alternative. To combat this, teams should aim for "perceived simplicity," where advanced features are easily discoverable for those who need them but effectively hidden for those who don't. This approach, exemplified by apps like WhatsApp, maintains a clean [[concepts/customer-experience|user experience]] for the majority while serving advanced needs.
