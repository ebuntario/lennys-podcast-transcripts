Leaders must actively run toward the conversations and decisions they fear most, such as firing an executive or rearchitecting a product. Hesitation creates a vacuum that causes organizational nervousness and political maneuvering. By confronting difficult choices head-on, you maintain control and prevent the dysfunction that arises from [[concepts/decision-making|decision-making]] paralysis.
