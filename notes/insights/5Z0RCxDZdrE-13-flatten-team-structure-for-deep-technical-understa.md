In fast-moving technical fields, keep teams small ("two-pizza teams") and flat. Leaders must stay deeply in the weeds of the technology to make good decisions and avoid armchair quarterbacking. When teams get too large, leaders lose touch with the technical realities, which is dangerous in a rapidly evolving space.
