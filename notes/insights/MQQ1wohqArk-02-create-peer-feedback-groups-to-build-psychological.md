Organizing small, cross-level peer groups that meet regularly to review early-stage work (like strategies or prototypes) builds trust and demystifies the feedback process. This practice allows team members to share rough drafts safely and models how to give constructive, uplifting input. It strengthens [[concepts/psychological-safety|psychological safety]] by making vulnerability and collaborative improvement a regular, low-stakes habit.
