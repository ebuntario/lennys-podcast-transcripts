The fastest path to learning how to solve a problem is to try solving it manually, outside your building, with real people. This [[concepts/doing-things-that-dont-scale|doing things that don't scale]] approach generates all the necessary evidence, reveals the difference between what people say and do, and validates that a problem is worth solving. It replaces assumptions with direct experience and failure.
