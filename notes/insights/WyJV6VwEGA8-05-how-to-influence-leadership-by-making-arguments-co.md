To influence leadership effectively, make your arguments concrete with artifacts, examples, and data. Leaders often ask follow-up questions to understand an issue from [[concepts/first-principles-thinking|first principles]], so be prepared to find answers to those questions. The process involves debating the concrete proposal until a shared understanding is reached, which prevents decisions from being bottlenecked by uncertainty.
