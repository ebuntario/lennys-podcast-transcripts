When a leader lacks confidence in a team's direction, the effective approach is not to be hands-off but to engage in [[concepts/selective-micromanagement|selective micromanagement]]. This involves temporarily taking detailed control to guide the team onto the correct path, with the explicit goal of pulling back once they understand the right direction. This tactical, temporary intervention prevents the team from going off the rails and helps establish the frameworks needed for future [[concepts/team-autonomy|autonomy]].
