A practical research method is the Van Westendorp survey, which asks four questions to find the optimal price point: at what price is it too cheap to be quality, a bargain, starting to get expensive, and prohibitively expensive. Plotting these responses helps marry feature demand with [[concepts/willingness-to-pay|customer willingness to pay]].
