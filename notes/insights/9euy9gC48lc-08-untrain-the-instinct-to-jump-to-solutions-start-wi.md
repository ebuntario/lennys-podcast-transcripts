A common mistake, especially for new product managers, is becoming overly attached to a specific solution before understanding the user problem. Effective [[concepts/product-management|product management]] requires retraining this instinct to first focus on users and their real-world challenges. The process should begin with understanding problems and identifying opportunities, rather than starting with a predetermined build plan, which often leads to misaligned and unsuccessful products.
