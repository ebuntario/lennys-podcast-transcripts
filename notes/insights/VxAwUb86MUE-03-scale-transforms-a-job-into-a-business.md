The key distinction between a job and a business is [[concepts/scale|scale]]: a solo operation is a job, but a venture that grows to employ others becomes a business. Entrepreneurs should practice "lazy leadership" by quickly [[concepts/automation|automating]] or delegating tasks they dislike, freeing themselves to focus on sales, strategy, or other high-leverage activities they enjoy. The goal is to build systems that allow the founder to step away from day-to-day execution.
