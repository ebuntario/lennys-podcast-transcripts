Traditional economics assumes rational, emotionless decision-making, but [[concepts/behavioral-economics|behavioral economics]] integrates psychology to show people make decisions with emotion in predictable patterns. Key predictable behaviors include [[concepts/present-bias|present bias]] (overweighting immediate needs) and following social norms, which allows for systematic behavior change when understood.
