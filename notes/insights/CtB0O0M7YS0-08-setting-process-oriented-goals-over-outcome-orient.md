Focus on goals you can directly control, like "publish one article per month," rather than outcomes you can't, like "gain 10,000 subscribers." [[concepts/process-over-outcome|Process-oriented goals]] build the consistent habits that lead to success, while outcome-oriented goals often lead to frustration because they depend on external factors. This mindset is applicable both in corporate careers and entrepreneurial ventures.
