A strong internal product community of practice increases employee stickiness and reduces the people development burden on individual managers. It's a cost-effective alternative to expensive trainings and coaches, as it leverages peer learning and sharing. Investing time in a [[concepts/community|community]] fosters mastery, keeps people engaged, and is a key factor in [[concepts/retention|retention]].
