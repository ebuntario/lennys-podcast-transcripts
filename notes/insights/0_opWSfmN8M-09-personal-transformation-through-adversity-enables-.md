Surviving professional failure, burnout, or public criticism can eviscerate a leader's ego, which is ultimately liberating. Combined with dedicated self-work (like therapy), this process removes counterproductive edges and insecurities. The result is greater [[concepts/self-awareness|self-awareness]], authentic communication, and the ability to lead from a place of clarity rather than ego.
