Product teams consist of a spectrum where one end holds "crazy innovators" who generate many ideas (most bad, but some game-changing) and the other holds strong "executors" who excel at turning a clear strategy into action. The ideal product manager sits in the middle, blending strategic ideation with robust [[concepts/strategy-execution|execution]]. Leaders often hire more executors but must actively develop their strategic skills to help them advance.
