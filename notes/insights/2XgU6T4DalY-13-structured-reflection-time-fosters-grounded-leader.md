Protecting daily time for unstructured [[concepts/self-reflection|reflection]]—such as early mornings for "puttering"—helps leaders stay grounded. This practice of checking in with your own energy, anxieties, and excitements is a muscle that improves [[concepts/leadership-development|leadership]] presence and decision-making. It allows you to understand what gives you energy and double down on those strengths.
