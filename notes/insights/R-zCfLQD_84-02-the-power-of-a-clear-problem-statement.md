Effective product development starts with spending significant time defining a nuanced problem statement. The goal is to reach a point where you can visualize the exact "mountain peak" you're aiming for and the "base camp" starting point. This clarity, rooted in [[concepts/first-principles-thinking|first principles]], enables focused execution and makes it possible to learn from outcomes, even if the initial solution is wrong.
