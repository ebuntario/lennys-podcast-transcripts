Research shows life satisfaction typically bottoms between ages 45-50, then steadily increases through subsequent decades. This U-curve pattern suggests that [[concepts/life-transitions|midlife transitions]] lead to greater happiness as people redefine success, shed unrealistic expectations, and gain perspective on what truly matters.
