Founders often fail when early mistakes cause them to lose confidence, leading to hesitation on subsequent decisions. This hesitation creates a power vacuum where senior executives begin making political moves to fill the void. The resulting [[concepts/organizational-dynamics|dysfunctional, political organization]] typically marks the point where the founder may need replacement.
