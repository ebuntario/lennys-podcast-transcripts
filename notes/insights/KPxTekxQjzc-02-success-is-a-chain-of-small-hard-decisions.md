Success is rarely a single brilliant move but a series of small, difficult decisions that don't seem impactful individually. The key is breaking the chain of compounding bad decisions by psychologically accepting [[concepts/sunk-cost-fallacy|sunk costs]] and making one good, hard choice. This good decision then enables the next, eventually leading to a positive outcome through cumulative effort.
