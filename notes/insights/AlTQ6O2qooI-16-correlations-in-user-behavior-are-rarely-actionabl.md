A common analytical failure is observing that your best users perform behavior X and concluding you can drive retention by making other users do X. This [[concepts/causation-vs-correlation|correlation]] is rarely causal; the behavior is usually a symptom of a unique customer circumstance or a deep product fit. Instead, focus on understanding and replicating the fundamental drivers of value in the early user experience.
