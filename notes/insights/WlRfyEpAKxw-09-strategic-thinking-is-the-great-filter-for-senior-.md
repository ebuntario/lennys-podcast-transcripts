The primary skill bottleneck preventing product managers from reaching director or CPO levels is the inability to formulate and drive [[concepts/organizational-product-strategy|product strategy]] independently. Early-career PMs prove themselves by shipping features that customers love, but advancement requires demonstrating they can write a strategy doc and drive decision-making without guidance. Investing in mentorship, training, and exposure to strategic thinkers is crucial to develop this skill.
