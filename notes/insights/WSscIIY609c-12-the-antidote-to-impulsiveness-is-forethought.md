Distraction is fundamentally an impulse control issue that can be managed through advance planning. By taking steps today to prevent distraction tomorrow—like scheduling time blocks or setting up effort pacts—you replace reactive impulsiveness with [[concepts/proactive-behavior|proactive forethought]]. This turns focus into a practiced skill rather than an innate talent.
