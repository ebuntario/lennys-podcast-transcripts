Core questions for self-inquiry include: "What am I not saying that I need to say?", "What am I saying that's not being heard?", "What's being said that I'm not hearing?", and the foundational complicity question. These questions are most valuable when they startle you or take your breath away, indicating you're confronting [[concepts/self-delusion|self-delusion]].
