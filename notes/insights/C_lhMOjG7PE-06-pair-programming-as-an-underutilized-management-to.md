[[concepts/pair-programming|Pair programming]] dramatically increases learning, reduces silos, and improves code quality despite appearing inefficient. Two people working together on one computer focus intensely on problem-solving rather than keystrokes, leading to more elegant solutions. Like the underhand free throw in basketball, it's statistically superior but often avoided because it "looks dumb," despite its proven benefits.
