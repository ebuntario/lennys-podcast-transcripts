It's important to distinguish product operations from project or program management. While program managers focus on large, ongoing company initiatives and project managers on time-boxed deliverables, product ops is fundamentally about increasing the speed and quality of [[concepts/strategic-work|strategic decision-making]]. Its work is centered on enabling the product organization through systems, insights, and processes, not on tracking task completion.
