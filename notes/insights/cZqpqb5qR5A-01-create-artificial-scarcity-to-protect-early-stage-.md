Large companies have a tendency to over-invest in new bets, which can slow down experimentation. To emulate a [[concepts/startup-mindset|startup environment]], you must create artificial scarcity and remind stakeholders that failure is the most likely outcome. This framing buys autonomy for the team to hack solutions, test concepts quickly, and avoid being dragged into lengthy platform or process discussions prematurely.
