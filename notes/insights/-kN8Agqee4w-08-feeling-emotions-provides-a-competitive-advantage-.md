Research shows that people who cannot feel emotions (like patient Elliot) become incapable of making basic decisions. Your body provides a stream of predictive data via [[concepts/somatic-markers|somatic markers]]. Ignoring emotions creates bias, as you'll subconsciously avoid decisions that might trigger uncomfortable feelings. Welcoming the full emotional spectrum leads to clearer, less biased choices.
