Encouraging pair programming and regular switching of pairs builds knowledge redundancy across the organization, preventing "single points of failure." This practice creates a safety net, making it easier for people to move to new projects without leaving critical systems unsupported. It also enhances [[concepts/team-resilience|team resilience]] and provides continuous learning opportunities for individuals.
