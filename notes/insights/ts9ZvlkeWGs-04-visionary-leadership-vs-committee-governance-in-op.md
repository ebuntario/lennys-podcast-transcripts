Great software that survives technological shifts often requires a clear [[concepts/product-strategy|visionary leader]] willing to make unpopular long-term bets, rather than governance by committee. While accountability exists through the community's ability to fork the project, a meritocratic hierarchy where contributors earn authority through work often leads to more decisive progress than purely democratic structures.
