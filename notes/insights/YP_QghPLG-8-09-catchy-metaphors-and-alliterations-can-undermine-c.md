Product leaders often gravitate toward ideas packaged in catchy metaphors like "two-way doors" or alliterations like "fail fast" without evaluating their substantive merit. This represents a [[concepts/cognitive-biases|cognitive bias]] that substitutes social proof for rigorous analysis. Developing taste involves separating conceptual value from packaging and authority associations.
