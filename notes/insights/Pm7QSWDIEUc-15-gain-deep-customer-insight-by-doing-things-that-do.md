In the early stages, founders should engage in deep, unscalable practices to understand their customers, like the Gusto founder who ran payroll with the first 200 customers to observe their reactions. This hands-on [[concepts/customer-insight|customer insight]] is the "secret sauce" for building a product people love and is harder to obtain as a company grows larger and more detached from end-users.
