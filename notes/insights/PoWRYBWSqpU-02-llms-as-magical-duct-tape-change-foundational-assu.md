Large Language Models act as "magical duct tape," fundamentally altering the [[concepts/cost-structure|cost structure]] of software by making creation cheaper and inference more expensive. This [[concepts/disruptive-technology|disruptive technology]] invalidates many mature industry playbooks, as it changes assumptions about what is possible to build and monetize. Product builders must now design for a world where software is squishy and not perfectly reliable.
