When addressing problematic behavior with high performers, frame the conversation around skill development rather than personal criticism. For example, tell an executive they're effective in their core function but need to develop cross-functional effectiveness to reach the next level. This [[concepts/management-communication|constructive framing]] preserves the relationship while clearly communicating necessary changes.
