Successful startups often survive multiple points where rationally they should have shut down. Founders need an irrational intention to keep going even when the world signals failure, as demonstrated by companies like Airbnb that persisted through repeated setbacks. This [[concepts/founder-resilience|founder resilience]] is a common thread among companies that later appear as overnight successes.
