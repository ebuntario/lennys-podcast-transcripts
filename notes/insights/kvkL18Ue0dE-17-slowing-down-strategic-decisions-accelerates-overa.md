Taking time for literature reviews, competitive analysis, and reflection before adopting methodologies or building products prevents costly rework. This deliberate [[concepts/decision-making|decision-making]] approach feels counterintuitive during urgent situations but ultimately creates faster, more effective execution by avoiding wrong paths. The most successful organizations balance urgency with thoughtful preparation.
