Pursuing [[concepts/internationalization|internationalization]] early forces a company to adapt its product for global markets, which can unlock faster growth trajectories. Localizing the product for different regions often reveals new use cases and platform requirements (e.g., mobile-first markets). This strategy not only expands the addressable market but also actively shapes the product roadmap.
