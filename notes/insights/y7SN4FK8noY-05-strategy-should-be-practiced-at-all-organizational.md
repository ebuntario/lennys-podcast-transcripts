Critical strategic choices are often made deep within an organization, not just at the top. Companies like P&G succeed by empowering brand managers to make strategic decisions, viewing them as future CEOs in training. The dichotomy between "strategy" (for leaders) and "strategy-execution" (for others) is harmful and limits organizational potential.
