In early-stage startups, the founder's core job is to ensure value and viability, which is the same as a product manager's role. Hiring a real product manager too soon creates conflict and can slow things down. A good heuristic is to wait until you have around 20-25 engineers, typically after achieving initial [[concepts/product-market-fit|product-market fit]] and scaling into new markets.
