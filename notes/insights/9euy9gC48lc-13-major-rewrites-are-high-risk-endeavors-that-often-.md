Undertaking a full rewrite of an existing product is fraught with risk, often leading to multi-year delays, failure to achieve feature parity, and sunk cost fallacy. These projects frequently fail because teams skip discovery, underestimate technical complexity, and lack a clear one-pager. The lesson is to avoid large-scale rewrites in favor of iterative improvements, as they rarely deliver the promised transformation and can stall [[concepts/product-development|product development]] for years.
