When OKRs fail, they typically reveal deeper organizational issues like lack of strategy, unclear decision-making, or poor psychological safety. Teams constantly asking for direction indicate unclear strategy, while fear of changing course suggests [[concepts/psychological-safety|psychological safety]] problems. OKRs serve as diagnostic tools that highlight where fundamental improvements are needed beyond the goal-setting process itself.
