When presenting ideas, explicitly separate known facts from your hypotheses and your plans to test them. This openness about uncertainty builds more credibility than projecting overconfidence, as it invites others to contribute their knowledge and perspectives. This approach fosters trust and better [[concepts/decision-making|collaborative decision-making]].
