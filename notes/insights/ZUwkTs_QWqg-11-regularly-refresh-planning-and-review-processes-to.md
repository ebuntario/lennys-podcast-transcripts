Processes like quarterly planning and product reviews tend to become bloated and inefficient over time. To combat this, leaders should deliberately [[concepts/continuous-improvement|change or tweak these processes every few quarters]]—for example, by shifting to top-down "big rock" prioritization or enforcing shorter review meetings. This prevents institutional creep and keeps the focus on clarity and speed.
