For distributed teams, solving complex, gnarly problems is significantly harder without a foundation of in-person trust. Periodically bringing the team together for 48-hour working sessions with a tight agenda can unlock decisions that would take weeks remotely. This in-person time builds the camaraderie and shared context that sustains effective [[concepts/remote-work|remote collaboration]].
