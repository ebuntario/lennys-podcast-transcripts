Product leaders often mistake feature decisions as reversible "two-way doors" when they actually create long-term commitments. Once a feature ships, organizational momentum and stakeholder expectations make it difficult to remove, leading to [[concepts/product-debt|product debt]] accumulation. Pausing to properly evaluate customer motivation and differentiation before building prevents this cycle of busywork.
