Macro barriers involve expanding access (e.g., supporting new languages or device types), while micro barriers involve removing small points of friction in user flows (e.g., simplifying email confirmation). For products with strong [[concepts/product-market-fit|product-market fit]], growth efforts can be highly effective by systematically identifying and eliminating these barriers to usage.
