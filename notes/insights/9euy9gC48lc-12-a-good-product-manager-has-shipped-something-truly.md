If a product manager hasn't shipped a terrible product, they likely haven't had enough experience or taken enough risks. The ability to openly admit and analyze a past failure is a strong indicator of maturity and learning. This perspective values reps and resilience over a perfect track record, recognizing that [[concepts/product-leadership|product management]] is iterative and built on lessons from both successes and significant missteps.
